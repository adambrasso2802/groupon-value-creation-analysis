# Groupon (GRPN) — Post-LBO Value Creation Plan
**Sponsor:** [NewCo Capital]  **Closing assumption:** 2025  **Hold:** 3 yrs  **Author:** Deal team

---

## Situation
Groupon is a once-dominant local-commerce marketplace that has lost ~45% of revenue and ~25% of active customers since 2019. The asset is small enough (~$500M revenue, ~$50M Adj. EBITDA run-rate exiting FY24) to be a credible take-private/sponsor target, with a recognised brand, durable Beauty/Spa/Health vertical, and a global merchant graph that no new entrant can rebuild de novo. The business has been run for survival, not growth, since 2020. There is a turnaround thesis.

## The 3 Biggest Opportunities
1. **Re-platform merchant economics.** The #1 merchant complaint (~92% frequency in public sentiment) is unsustainable take-rate at the 50% / 50/50 standard deal. Introduce **tiered discount floors (20–30% category)** plus **7-day single-tranche payouts**. Expected: merchant churn -20%, new-merchant sign-ups +30%, gross-profit margin +100–150bps over 18 months.
2. **Add a subscription / loyalty layer.** Comparable PE plays (Bumble, Trainline, MyHeritage, Ebates) re-rated 1.5–2x on a recurring-revenue narrative. Relaunch **Groupon Select** with a clear, non-dark-pattern opt-in and bolt on a merchant-side **"Loyalty Connect"** that converts voucher buyers into full-price repeat customers (the #2 merchant complaint).
3. **Reactivate inventory in the top 25 US DMAs + selective category re-entry into Goods.** Local sales coverage has been gutted; competitor Wowcher demonstrates Goods is still ~25-30% take-rate margin-accretive. Adds ~$30-50M revenue at >40% incremental contribution.

## The 3 Biggest Risks
1. **Demand-side brand decay.** Trustpilot sits in the "Bad" tier; customer net sentiment ~24/100. Reactivating merchants without fixing refunds / fine-print / availability sync will accelerate, not stabilise, churn. Mitigation: ringfence $15M for a CX overhaul before any growth marketing spend.
2. **Disintermediation by full-stack competitors.** Viator has won Things-to-Do; Booking owns Getaways; Fresha/Booksy own Beauty bookings. Groupon's marketplace shrinks if it remains a coupon site. Mitigation: pivot positioning from "discount" to "local discovery + booking."
3. **Levered capital structure into a declining top-line.** Even at modest LBO leverage (5–6x), a Bear case (–5 to –10% revenue p.a.) compresses fixed-charge coverage fast. Mitigation: front-load cost-out (target 600–800bps EBITDA margin lift Y1), maintain revolver capacity, covenant-lite preferred.

## 100-Day Plan
**Days 0–30 — Diagnose & lock**
- Stand up new Board (sponsor + 2 operating partners ex-marketplace).
- Hire CRO and Chief Merchant Officer. CEO assessment by Day 60.
- Build merchant-cohort LTV/CAC by category from internal data; freeze unprofitable cohorts.
- Customer-side: triage Trustpilot, publish 48-hour refund SLA.

**Days 31–60 — Reset economics**
- Roll out tiered discount floors + 7-day payouts in 3 pilot DMAs (Chicago, LA, NY).
- Kill 2–3 sub-scale international markets; reinvest savings in US/UK/CA.
- Renegotiate top-20 payment processor / cloud / SEM contracts (target $20M run-rate save).

**Days 61–100 — Build the growth engine**
- Relaunch Groupon Select with transparent opt-in; target 1.5M paid subs by exit.
- Sign 2 inventory-partnership LOIs (one OTA for Getaways, one experiences aggregator for Activities).
- Begin Goods relaunch design; soft-launch by Day 120.
- Set 12-quarter KPI dashboard cadence (this dashboard) with sponsor monthly ops review.

## Expected Outcomes (vs Public Comps)
| Case | 2027 EBITDA | Exit Multiple | Implied EV |
|---|---|---|---|
| Bear | ~$36M | 6–8x distressed | ~$220–290M |
| **Base** | **~$82M** | **10–12x sponsor-to-sponsor** | **~$815–980M** |
| Bull | ~$142M | 14–18x re-list w/ sub narrative | ~$2.0–2.5B |

Base case implies ~2.5x MoM on a $300–350M equity cheque assuming 5x net leverage at close. Bull case is the prize: convert Groupon from "coupon site" to "local-discovery subscription marketplace." Bear case is contained by aggressive cost-out and asset-sale optionality (Goods, international units).
