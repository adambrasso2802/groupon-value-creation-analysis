# Groupon (GRPN) — Post-LBO Value Creation Plan (REVISED 2026-05)
**Sponsor:** [NewCo Capital]  **Closing assumption:** 2026  **Hold:** 3 yrs  **Author:** Deal team

> **Material revision:** The prior memo (dated pre-Q1-25) described Groupon as "run for survival" and built a 2027 base case of ~$82M Adj. EBITDA. The 2025 actuals show a turnaround is already underway — FY24 Adj. EBITDA closed at **$69.3M** (vs $50.9M in the prior memo's hand-built quarterly stack), and NA Local gross billings grew **+11% / +20% / +18% YoY** across Q1/Q2/Q3-25 with active customers re-growing sequentially (**15.4M → 15.8M → 16.1M**). The thesis is no longer rescue-and-stabilise; it is acceleration-and-re-rate. See CHANGE_LOG.md for line-by-line revisions.

---

## Situation (revised)
Groupon is a global local-commerce marketplace that **has already inflected** under current management. FY24 revenue of $492.6M and Adj. EBITDA of $69.3M represent ~14% EBITDA margin — close to the prior memo's full 3-year base-case exit margin. The 2025 print shows the NA Local franchise (the engine of the business) reaccelerating into double-digit billings growth while customer count rebuilds for the first time since 2019. The asset is no longer a melting ice cube; it is an underappreciated marketplace re-rating in real time. PE entry is therefore competing against an improving public comp rather than a distressed one, but the upside in the Bull case is materially larger than originally modelled.

## The 3 Biggest Opportunities (revised)
1. **Compound the NA Local recovery into the rest of the portfolio.** The 18% Q3-25 billings growth is concentrated in NA Local — Goods, Getaways, and International still trail. Replicate the NA Local playbook (sales coverage, take-rate flexibility) into UK and the top 5 European markets; selectively re-enter Goods now that the core is healthy. *Was prior opp #3 ("reactivate top-25 DMAs"); has been partially executed already.*
2. **Add a subscription / loyalty layer to lock in re-rate.** With customers re-growing and merchants re-engaging, a credible recurring-revenue narrative (Groupon Select v2 + merchant-side Loyalty Connect) is the single largest multiple lever. Comparable PE plays (Bumble, Trainline, Etsy) re-rated 1.5–2x on subscription mix shifts. Target 1.5–2.0M paid subs by exit. *Largely unchanged from prior memo; valuation impact now bigger because base is bigger.*
3. **Convert volume growth into margin.** Q1–Q3 2025 take-rate (revenue / billings) sits at 30.3% / 30.2% / 29.5% — slight compression suggests volume is masking marginal take-rate erosion. Tiered category floors (20–30% off rather than 50%-only), faster merchant payouts, and re-segmenting account coverage should add 100–200bps of gross margin without slowing growth. *Diligence priority: confirm whether management has already begun this.*

## The 3 Biggest Risks (revised)
1. **Disintermediation by full-stack competitors (still primary).** Viator owns Things-to-Do, Booking owns Getaways, Fresha/Booksy own Beauty bookings. Local recovery has not yet extended into Getaways and the gap there is widening. *Unchanged.*
2. **Brand decay and CX (downgraded from #1).** Trustpilot still in the "Bad" tier and consumer net sentiment remains low — but the customer count is growing in spite of it, which materially weakens the prior thesis that brand was the binding constraint. Reframe: brand is a margin and multiple drag, not a survival risk. Ringfence $10M (down from $15M) for CX work and stop describing Groupon as "discount-only."
3. **Volume-masking-margin (new).** Take-rate compression while billings accelerate would mean management has bought growth with discount giveaways. If true, the EBITDA story unwinds in 2026 as the easy comps fade. Highest diligence priority before signing.

*De-emphasised vs prior memo:* the "levered capital structure into a declining top-line" risk. Top-line is no longer declining. Leverage capacity is in fact larger.

## 100-Day Plan (revised — some items already in motion)
**Days 0–30 — Diagnose & lock**
- Stand up new Board (sponsor + 2 operating partners ex-marketplace).
- CEO assessment by Day 60. **Stronger presumption of continuity** than the prior memo: incumbent management has delivered the inflection.
- Confirm or rebut margin diagnostic (take-rate vs volume mix). This is the single most important Day-30 deliverable.
- Build merchant-cohort LTV/CAC by category from internal data.

**Days 31–60 — Reset where it has not already reset**
- Audit which of {tiered floors, 7-day payouts, sales-coverage changes} are already deployed in NA Local. **Do not re-launch what management is already running.**
- Roll the NA Local playbook into UK + one EU market.
- Kill 2–3 sub-scale international markets that did not participate in the 2025 recovery; reinvest savings.
- Renegotiate top-20 payment processor / cloud / SEM contracts ($20M run-rate save).

**Days 61–100 — Build the re-rate engine**
- Launch Groupon Select v2 with transparent opt-in; target 1.5M paid subs by exit.
- Sign 2 inventory-partnership LOIs (one OTA for Getaways, one experiences aggregator).
- Begin Goods relaunch design; soft-launch by Day 120.
- Refresh investor narrative — reposition company from "coupon site" to "local-discovery marketplace re-rating on growth + subscription."

**Where the prior memo was materially wrong:**
- Prior Bear exit EV ($220–290M) assumed a distressed asset. With FY24 EBITDA already at $69M and 2025 growing, even a stall-out exits at ~$550–700M. The downside has been substantially de-risked.
- Prior Base exit EV ($815–980M) on $82M EBITDA. The actual 2027 base case is now $110M+ EBITDA on a higher growth multiple → ~$1.4–1.7B. Equity returns are ~2× the prior model.
- Prior Bull exit EV ($2.0–2.5B) on $142M EBITDA was the right shape but understated; current Bull is ~$2.7–3.3B.
- The "brand decay accelerates churn" risk was overweight — customers are growing in spite of brand drag.
- The "100-day plan" partially assumed levers (sales coverage, NA Local revival) that current management appears to have already pulled.

**Strategic implication:** at the previous entry assumption ($300–350M equity check, 5x leverage), the new Base case implies ~3.5–4× MoM rather than 2.5×. The principal risk for the sponsor is now overpaying at entry on a price set by competitive auction, not getting the operating plan wrong.

## Workstream 4: App & CX Overhaul *(elevated 2026-05; companion to `groupon-redesign/`)*

**Investment case.** Groupon's 4.8-star iOS rating is a sampling artifact of a closed in-app complaint funnel — every other review surface sits at 1.5/5 (Reviews.io 9.7K reviews, 7% would recommend; PissedConsumer 28.1K reviews; BBB ~1.0) — and three of the four dominant complaints (refund trap, invisible support, broken post-payment booking) sit directly on retention, not brand. With FY25 billings growing ~18% YoY against active customers growing only ~4.5% over three quarters (15.4M → 16.1M), the recovery is being carried by existing cohorts; the Bull-case Select v2 subscription thesis (~$2.7–3.3B EV) cannot underwrite 1.5–2.0M paid subs without first repairing the post-purchase trust contract that determines whether those subs renew.

**KPIs (drawn from the redesign's nine-tier architecture).**
1. **Off-platform rating delta** — close the gap between iOS App Store rating and Reviews.io / Trustpilot / PissedConsumer composite by 1.5 stars over 24 months. Instrumented by Tier 1 analytics and Tier 8's `PostPurchaseHelpEntry` intercept rate (% of refund-eligible frustrated sessions resolved in-app vs. escalated externally).
2. **Post-payment booking success rate** — % of bookable-service purchases that result in a confirmed merchant slot within 24 hours. Target ≥95% (vs. an inferred current rate well below that based on "circle keeps spinning" complaint density). Driven by Tier 6's `AvailabilityCalendar` + atomic booking integration.
3. **New-customer 90-day retention and Select v2 attach rate among new cohorts** — the only KPIs that prove the CX fix is converting first-time buyers (the visible weakness in the billings-vs-customers wedge) into the recurring-revenue base that underwrites the exit multiple. Driven by Tiers 2/6/7 (refund badge, plain-English fine print, single-screen checkout) plus Tier 9 notification taper.

**Owner.** A **VP, Customer Experience & Retention** reporting to the CPO, with dotted-line accountability to the CFO for refund P&L and to the COO for merchant booking-integration coverage. This role does not currently exist in the inferred org structure; the deal team should make standing it up a Day-1 hire, not a Day-100 search.

**100-day milestone.** Ship Tiers 1, 2, 6, and 8 of the redesign (analytics foundation, refund/BNPL badges, deal-detail trust contract, post-purchase support entry) to 100% of NA iOS traffic — using the existing 50% rebuilt-app rollout as the deployment vehicle — with the off-platform rating delta, booking success rate, and post-purchase support deflection rate reported weekly to the Board's AI committee.

**Budget reframing.** The prior memo's $10M CX ringfence was sized as defensive brand spend. Reframe as a $15–20M product-and-engineering programme over 24 months with explicit EBITDA attribution (refund-rate reduction × billings; Select v2 attach lift × ARPU). At Base-case sub-economics, every 100 bps of new-cohort retention is worth materially more than the entire programme cost.
